What is Diceware?

Diceware is a method for creating passphrases, passwords, and other cryptographic variables using ordinary dice as a hardware random number generator. Every four or five digit number 1,111 to 6,666 and 11,111 to 66,666 corresponds to an unique English word. Each digit is determined by a 'dice roll' or a randomly generated number from 1 to 6. A completed diceware password consists of multiple words randomly generated using this method.

Why Diceware?

Diceware makes memorable passwords that are also more secure than average passwords. When a person writes their own password, they may try to pick 'random' words, but in reality these words are usually not very random and therefore easy to guess. However, since Diceware generate truly random words, it is harder to crack than the average password.
